Backstory

Backstory is an app that allows users to view a succinct timeline of a news story's events in a single view. Created by myself and three others over 36 hours at a Society for News Design hackathon. It's cross-device compatible, and could be especially useful if you find yourself in a "Castaway"-type situation. More info about the process here.

North by Northwestern Redesign (in-progress, exp. completion 4/1)

A three-month project redesigning the website of Northwestern's magazine, North By Northwestern. Extensive iterating and wireframing in InDesign/Photoshop/pen & paper for all device sizes, considerable typography research and hand-coding each page from scratch. A true learning experience. Links to in-progress Home and Article pages.

Barreling Ahead

My capstone project at Medill. Was tasked with producing a one-page, immersive, fully interactive story. The topic of choice was the craft beer industry. While all students were required to use Bootstrap as a base, I hand-coded the remainder of the site. Discovered the greatness of jQuery's smooth scroll, and can now hold my own in conversations with most beer snobs.

Jay Cutler Screen Crack

An experimentation with CSS3 animation that speaks to Jay Cutler's arm strength. Hover over the football for the effect. And wherever your volume is right now, cut it in half.

Cinemagraphs

I created cinemagraphs in my spare time during my final quarter at Medill, with the intention of using them in the "Barreling Ahead" interactive story. Learned multiple techniques in both Adobe Photoshop and After Effects.

Journalism!

My extensive writing background includes positions (at) and work (for) a variety of prominent sports and lifestyle publications and websites, including UrbanDaddy (Boston & Chicago editions), The Improper Bostonian and Bleacher Report. I've been featured in USA Today, Yahoo! Sports and CBS, and currently write weekly columns for BaseballHQ.com. I did a bit of reporting at Medill as well.

About

A mentor once told that me in order to become skilled at anything, you have to try it, and you have to be bad at it. That was my barrier into design and development for years. Many sleepless nights and duct-tape websites later, I've developed a considerable skill-set in code to complement a strong background in journalism. I'm obsessive about typography, code semantically in both HTML5 and div-based layouts and am constantly experimenting with alternative page designs. I also prefer amber beers and am a fantasy sports junkie. Below you'll see I'm also on the social media, and have regularly updated Github and Dribbble accounts.
